HUSBAND & WIFE WEEKLY MEETING – STEP-BY-STEP GUIDE

This is a simple, structured time for you and your spouse to reconnect spiritually, emotionally, and practically. Set aside 30–60 minutes in a quiet, distraction-free space. Treat this like a real date—choose a comfortable spot, whether that's a cozy café, a park bench, or your own living room. If you're staying home, make it special: prepare a nice meal, light some candles, or at least have coffee and snacks ready so you both feel cared for from the very start. Follow these six parts in order.


PART 1 – OPENING PRAYER

The husband is to lead this prayer every single time. This is his opportunity to cover their time together in humility and invite God's presence. He should pray for openness, honesty, and unity between them. Surrender the meeting to the Lord and ask Him to speak to both of you. Keep it simple and heartfelt—this is just to center your hearts on Him before you dive in.


PART 2 – AFFIRMATION, LOOK BACK & THANKS

Take turns looking back over the past week. Share specific things you appreciated about your spouse—actions, words, or attitudes that meant something to you. This is not about criticism; it's about gratitude. Thank each other for the ways you showed up, whether in big responsibilities or small kindnesses. The goal is to start with encouragement and remind each other that you see and value one another's efforts.


PART 3 – MESSAGE FROM THE LORD

Each of you should come prepared with something the Lord has been speaking to you personally during your own prayer and scripture reading over the past week. Share what you've been learning, any verses that stood out, or any promptings you've felt. This isn't a sermon—it's simply sharing what God is doing in your own heart. Listen to each other without interrupting or fixing. Let this be a time to grow together in faith by hearing what He's saying to each of you.


PART 4 – CONNECTION QUESTIONS

Go through these five questions one at a time. Take turns answering each one fully before moving to the next. Be honest, listen without judgment, and resist the urge to problem-solve—especially for the harder questions. This is about understanding each other's hearts.

1. "Where have you seen God at work in your life this week?"
   This keeps our friendship rooted in faith—not just logistics and schedules.
   It reminds us we're both growing people, not just teammates running a household.

2. "What was your favorite part of the week?"
   This helps us practice gratitude and hear what actually lights each other up.
   It's an easy entry point when conversation feels flat.

3. "What's something you're proud of lately?"
   It's a chance to cheer each other on for things that may not get noticed otherwise—in work, in parenting, in spiritual growth, in self-discipline.

4. "What's been feeling heavy or frustrating for you?"
   An open door to vent, share honestly, and feel heard—without fixing.
   This question has prevented so many silent resentments in our marriage.

5. "Is there one thing I could do this week to help you feel supported?"
   Sometimes it's practical. Sometimes it's emotional.
   But asking builds trust and reminds us we're both showing up for each other.


PART 5 – SCHEDULE THE WEEK AHEAD

Pull out your calendars and look at the coming week together. Review any appointments, commitments, meals, or family needs. Coordinate logistics so you're on the same page. Decide who is handling what, and flag any potential stress points so you can face them as a team. This prevents last-minute surprises and keeps your household running smoothly.


PART 6 – PRAY FOR ONE ANOTHER

Close your meeting by praying over each other for the week ahead. Hold hands or lay a hand on your spouse's shoulder. Pray specifically for the things you just shared—the burdens, the hopes, the practical needs, and the spiritual growth. Ask God to go before you both and to strengthen your marriage. You can do this right where you are, or if you prefer, take it to the car during a quiet drive or save it for when you get back home—wherever you both feel most at ease. End with a sense of unity and peace, knowing you've brought everything to Him together.
